# Data Dictionary: Treatment of Lead-Exposed Children (TLC) Trial

This study is a placebo-controlled, randomized study of succimer (a chelating agent) in children with blood lead levels of 20-44 micrograms/dL. The data consist of four repeated measurements of blood lead levels obtained at baseline (or week 0), week 1, week 4, and week 6 on 100 children who were randomly assigned to chelation treatment with succimer or placebo. 

*Note: The `sex` and `age` variables in this dataset were simulated for the purposes of this project, and the generated ages extend beyond the actual age range of the original study participants.*

| Variable Name | Type        | Description                                                                 | Missingness |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| `ID`          | Integer     | A unique subject identifier                                                 | None        |
| `Group`       | Categorical | Subject group: 'A' (Active) or 'P' (Placebo)                                | None        |
| `Week0`       | Numeric     | Baseline blood lead level                                                   | None        |
| `Week1`       | Numeric     | Blood lead level after 1 week                                               | None        |
| `Week4`       | Numeric     | Blood lead level after 4 weeks                                              | None        |
| `Week6`       | Numeric     | Blood lead level after 6 weeks                                              | None        |
| `sex`         | Categorical | Simulated patient sex (e.g., 'male', 'female')                              | None        |
| `age`         | Numeric     | Simulated patient age (extends beyond the ages of the actual study's kids)  | None        |
